Before I ever wrote a line of code, I was a cricket player. The discipline, iterative improvement, and patience under pressure I learned on the field still shape how I think. During my undergraduate degree, I took a Python course out of curiosity. What began as a side interest became a turning point. It introduced me to a new way of thinking built on logic, creativity, and endless possibility.

That spark led me to ExploreAI, where I began my career in data. Today, I work as a Data Analyst and spend most of my free time building AI applications end-to-end, embracing the messiness of learning in practice.

I started this blog as a place to learn: to improve how I write, reason, and build. This is where I share what I'm working through. It’s a space to explore and express ideas that interest me. I'm learning as I go and welcome feedback that helps me grow.

If you come from an unconventional background or are driven by curiosity, I hope these experiments inspire you to start asking your own questions and creating, too.
